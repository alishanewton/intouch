Script Sections

  • Opening
  • Questions
  • Vocabulary and Listening
  • Sentence Making
  • Closing

Opening

(1 minute)

Hello! May I speak with [student’s name]?

This is [teacher’s name] and I’m calling you today for your scheduled level test.

I will talk to you for 10 minutes. Just try to answer my questions using complete sentences. Are you ready? 

Questions

(2 minutes)

For the first part of the test, I want to get to know you better so can you tell me:

  • Do you have an English name that I can call you?
  • How old are you? 
  • What is your job? 
  • What is the name of your company?
  • How long have you been working there? 
  • Is it your first time to have a phone class?
  • How long have you been studying English?

Vocabulary and Listening

(3 minutes)

We will now proceed to the next part, Vocabulary and Listening. In this part, I will give you a sentence and you will try to identify the correct words among the choices.

For vocabulary, ask the question as written below:
  1. This means your boss at work. Is it A, coworker, B, company, or C, supervisor?
  2. This pertains to the people you work with. Is it A, business, B, colleague, or C, schedule?
  3. This is the place where you study after high school. a. university b. company c. residence
  4. This is the main course of study in the university. a. activity b. major c. professor
  5. This refers to your mother and father. a. cousins b. relatives c. parents
  6. This is the place where your family comes from a. hospital b. hometown c. vacation

Okay. Now this time, I will give you an English word and you will tell me what it means.

For definition, pick one word from the lists above.

Sentence Making

(3 minutes)

The next part is called “sentence making.” I will ask you 1 or 2 questions and I want you to answer them in as much detail as possible. Please remember that there is no right or wrong answer to the questions I will ask you.

Pick just one or two of the following questions to ask.
  1. How do you maintain your health?
  2. What was the most challenging experience you ever had?
  3. Describe your hometown in as much detail as possible.
  4. What do you think is the most difficult job in the world? Why?
  5. Do you think you are successful?

Closing

(1 minute)

This is the end of our level test today. Thank you for your time. You can check the website to see the result of the level test. I hope you had a good experience today and I’m looking forward to having class with you soon.

Goodbye!
