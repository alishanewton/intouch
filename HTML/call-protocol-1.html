Dialing Protocol

Calling your students on time is crucial. Students expect to be called at the time listed on your schedule.

In this example, the student expects to be called at 19:00 Korea Standard Time (KST) or GMT+9. 

One exception to this rule is when you have back-to-back classes listed on your schedule, in which case you may call up to 30 seconds before the scheduled start time.

In this example, it is appropriate to call the first student 30 seconds earlier because the second class is starting immediately afterward. This will allow you to finish your call in time to call the second student on-time.

Class Length Guidelines

For each of these instances, please leave a note in the class memo:

Over Time: The class was one or more minutes in excess.

For example, the call was 11 minutes and 20 seconds.

Under Time: The class was 45 seconds less than the scheduled duration.

For example:

  • 10-minute class: 9m 14s or less
  • 15-minute class: 14m 14s or less
  • 20-minute class: 19m 14s or less

Late Call: You called late by 30 seconds or more. 

Calls that are more than 2 minutes late should be reported in the staff notice board/write board in addition to the class memo.

For CREDU students, you must make a fourth call in a 10-minute class. For all other students, only three calls are necessary.

If the student has a secondary number, call it when you make your last call. If the student doesn’t have a secondary number, call the primary number again.

Voicemail

After dialing, wait until you hear an automated voice message. This could take up to 75 seconds. Listen to this recording for an example. 

On your last call, leave a voicemail. For example: 

"Hi, this is Teacher Sam. I called you 3 times (or 4 times) with no answer. I hope we can meet for our next class. Bye!" 

Short Class

The student may sometimes request a short class for whatever reason, whether they are in a noisy public place or hoping to get back to a staff meeting. In this case, they need only to participate in class for 30% of their scheduled class time (50% for CREDU students). 

If the student requests to cancel class, inform them that they will be considered present if they complete at least 30% of their class time (50% for CREDU students). 

The goal is to have students be present for class. 

SHORT CLASS GUIDELINES
  • 10-minute class: at least 3m or 5m for CREDU 
  • 15-minute class: at least 5m or 8m for CREDU
  • 20-minute class: at least 6m or 10m for CREDU

In many cases, by the time the student answers and explains that they need to cancel class, you have already been speaking for 1-2 minutes. 

Consecutive Classes

On your schedule, you may see two or more consecutive classes with a student.

If you are having class, you don't need to hang up and call back. However, if the student doesn't answer for the first class, you have to call the student again for the next class according to the dialing protocol.
